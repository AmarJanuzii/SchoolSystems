Transforming Schools in Rwanda: An Inspiring Story

April 6, 2023

Rwanda's education system has undergone a significant transformation in recent years, and the results are truly inspiring. After the country's genocide in 1994, Rwanda's government made education a top priority in its efforts to rebuild the country.

One of the key initiatives was the establishment of the Nine-Year Basic Education (9YBE) program, which aims to provide universal access to education for all Rwandan children. The program includes six years of primary education and three years of lower secondary education.

To support the implementation of the 9YBE program, Rwanda introduced a number of measures to improve the quality of education. One such measure was the establishment of teacher training colleges across the country to ensure that teachers were well-prepared to teach the new curriculum.

The government also invested in new school infrastructure, including the construction of classrooms, libraries, and laboratories. In addition, Rwanda introduced a new curriculum that emphasizes critical thinking and problem-solving skills..

As a result of these initiatives, Rwanda has made significant progress in improving access to education and the quality of education. According to the World Bank, the primary school completion rate in Rwanda increased from 70% in 2000 to 95% in 2018. The enrollment rate for lower secondary education also increased from 16% in 2000 to 41% in 2018.

Moreover, Rwanda's efforts have not gone unnoticed on the international stage. In 2017, the World Innovation Summit for Education (WISE) awarded Rwanda the WISE Prize for Education in recognition of the country's progress in improving education.

Rwanda's education transformation is a remarkable success story, demonstrating how a strong commitment to education and targeted investment can lead to significant improvements in access and quality of education.
